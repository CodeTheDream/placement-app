About

Purpose

The Home Page is a browser-based web application for CRH social workers to find and exchange information about licensed mental health facilities in North Carolina. The purpose of the project is to improve the quality of information available to social workers with the goal of reducing time spent filtering through the list and calling potential placements. This will hopefully result in patients spending less time waiting for placement to be found, and being matched with placements that are a good fit for them (and vice versa).

The app allows social workers to view basic details about homes and their license-holders/referral contacts, make comments on homes to note basic information or describe the quality of the home, add announcements about more time-sensitive matters like bed availability, add homes to their personal shopping cart, and download their cart to an Excel spreadsheet for note-taking as they call homes on behalf of patients.

FAQ

How is this supposed to help me find placements?

Conduct a search by typing the full name of a county of city into the search bar. Browse placements, view comments, and add placements to your shopping cart that seem suitable for your patient. You can also add all search results to your cart using the button at the top of the search results page as well (if you want a list with all placements in a particular county or group of counties, for example). Once you have the placements in your cart that you plan on calling, you can download your cart into an Excel spreadsheet which will be pre-formatted with space to take notes about the calls you make and information you learn while conducting your search. You will still need to call potential placements to determine availability and refer your patient, but this tool will hopefully provide you with better information to cut down on your search time.

Go to the Announcements page to see a list of the most recent updates that have been posted about bed availability. When you hear about a placement with an opening, please post it there!

What’s in the database?

As of the most recent update, 5600A group home placements statewide are available in the database. The information is obtained from the DHSR licensed facilities listing. Some details have also been pulled from existing CRH “Recommended Placements” documents. Additional placement types will be added; please be patient as this process takes some time. The database is organized with separate categories for licensees and placements to help group together locations that have a single referral contact.

The administrator will also update existing information based on comments and announcements made by users like you. When you learn a new piece of information about a placement or licensee, please notify the administrator via one of the methods described below.

Is this site secure?

The website is secured using a variety of methods including HTTPS and access limitations built into the source code. Users cannot self-register, they can only be added by an administrator. The site is only accessible by CRH social workers who have requested credentials.

How do I add my updates to the site?

You can add comments about individual placements by navigating to the page for that placement and scrolling down to the comments section. Especially helpful would be the gender served at the facility, and the contact person for referrals along with their phone/fax numbers (especially if this is different than what is listed on the page). Consider other info like special funding needed, specific age range or diagnosis, HUD or other application types, or special populations that will/will not be considered (sex offenders, legal background).

You can also make announcements about bed openings on the Announcements page. Adding the placement and license-holder names are optional.

If you’ve used a downloaded spreadsheet to take notes and have logged bits of info about a lot of placements which would be tedious to go back and add individual comments about, feel free to email your spreadsheet to Lauren Sheridan and she will happily add your information to the database during the next round of updates.

Thank you in advance for giving back to your fellow SWs by adding updated info to the site!
